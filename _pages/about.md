I am a Ph.D. Candidate in the Charles H. Dyson School of Applied Economics and Management at [Cornell University](https://www.cornell.edu/). As an applied microeconomist, my research interests lie in the intersection of Demographic Economics and Environmental Economics where I explore the impact of climatic variations on individual and household level decisions. My latest [research](research) look into the impact of destination and origin specific weather conditions on the migratory decisions of agricultural workers' households, the impact of minimum temperature variations on fertility, and the interactions between perceived air quality and household consumption patterns.
